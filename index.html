Background

What is Geothermal Energy?

Harnessing Earth's Natural Heat

Geothermal energy is heat stored within the Earth, originating from the planet's formation and ongoing radioactive decay. It is harnessed via naturally occurring pockets of hot water and steam deep underground — called geothermal reservoirs.

Background Science

Earth's Internal Heat Structure

From Crust to Core

Earth's Crust (Lithosphere)

5–70 km deep. Temperature rises ~25–30°C/km. The main target zone for geothermal wells — accessible via modern drilling technology.

Upper Mantle

Reaches 1,000°C+. Convection currents transfer heat upward, driving plate tectonics and geothermal activity at the surface.

Geothermal Reservoirs

Porous rock saturated with hot water (80–350°C). Found near faults and volcanic zones — the primary drilling target.

Lebanon's Advantage

The Dead Sea Transform fault elevates gradients to 35–45°C/km — well above the global average of 25°C/km — making binary plants viable.

Background Science

Types of Geothermal Power Plants

Dry Steam · Flash Steam · Binary Cycle

Dry Steam

Oldest Method

Uses steam directly from underground fractures to drive a turbine. Requires reservoirs producing dry or superheated steam — the rarest reservoir type worldwide.

Required Temp> 150°C
Global Share~25% of plants
Example SiteThe Geysers, CA

Not suitable for Lebanon — requires rare dry steam reservoirs

Flash Steam

Most Common

High-pressure hot water is rapidly depressurized, flashing into steam that drives a turbine. Cooled fluid is reinjected into the reservoir.

Required Temp> 180°C
Global Share~65% of plants
Example SiteReykjavik, Iceland

Marginal for Lebanon — most Lebanese regions don't reach 180°C at viable depths

Binary Cycle

Best for Lebanon RECOMMENDED

Hot geothermal water heats a secondary working fluid (e.g. isobutane) via a heat exchanger. Fluid vaporizes in a fully closed loop — zero direct emissions or water loss.

Required Temp100–180°C ✓
Global Share~10% (fastest growing)
Example SiteNevada, New Zealand

Ideal for Lebanon — works at moderate temperatures, fully closed-loop, zero atmospheric emissions

Scientific Basis

Hypothesis

Energy Conversion Concept

Project Hypothesis

"If pipes are drilled into geothermally active regions, heat from the Earth's interior can produce steam that rotates a turbine, thereby generating electricity through a clean, non-polluting process."

1. Access Heat

Drill wells into geothermally active regions to reach subsurface hot rock reservoirs

2. Heat Fluid

Subsurface heat converts water into high-pressure steam or superheated fluid

3. Drive Turbine

Kinetic energy from expanding steam rotates the turbine at high speed

4. Generate Power

Turbine drives the generator, producing clean AC electricity for the grid

5. Reinject

Cooled fluid is reinjected into the reservoir to maintain pressure cycle

Monitoring & Assessment Metrics

4 Core Data Categories — Systematic Collection Framework

Geological & Geophysical

Subsurface Mapping

Geological Mapping: Rock formations and fault system analysis

Seismic Imaging: Reflection data to locate geothermal reservoirs

Heat Flow: Geothermal gradient measurements (°C/km)

Permeability: Porosity analysis of core rock samples

Reservoir Characterization

Fluid & Rock Properties

Exploratory Drilling: Direct temperature & pressure measurement

Fluid Chemistry: Isotope and mineral content analysis

Well Logging: Gamma ray, resistivity, porosity logs

Core Sampling: Direct rock property validation

Energy Production

Performance Metrics

Flow Rate: Mass flow of steam/water (kg/s) measurement

Thermodynamics: Temperature and enthalpy at wellhead

Gross Output: Estimated gross electrical output (MW)

Pressure Decline: Reservoir drawdown monitoring

Environmental & Economic

Impact & Feasibility

Environmental Baseline: Air and water quality monitoring

Seismic Monitoring: Micro-seismicity during operation

Impact Assessment: Land use, noise and community impact

Financials: CAPEX, OPEX, and LCOE analysis

Next Steps

Implementation Roadmap

5-Year Plan for Lebanon Geothermal

5-Year Phased Implementation — Lebanon Geothermal Development

Year 1

Phase 1

Geological Assessment & Feasibility

Immediate

Comprehensive geological surveys, seismic imaging, and heat flow mapping across all 5 priority zones. Commission UNDP-CEDRO collaboration for funding and expertise.

Year 1–2

Phase 2

Exploratory Drilling — Yammouneh

Priority

Drill 2 slim-hole test wells at Yammouneh Fault Zone. Directly measure subsurface temperature, pressure, and fluid chemistry at 2–3 km depth.

Year 2–3

Phase 3

Pilot Binary Plant — 1–5 MW

Investment

Design and construct a small-scale binary cycle plant at the best-performing site. Connect to local grid for live performance data and public demonstration.

Year 3–4

Phase 4

Optimization & Expansion Study

Scale-Up

Analyze pilot data. Identify additional drill sites in Akkar and Bekaa. Prepare investment case for 20–50 MW full-scale commercial development.

Year 4–5

Phase 5

Full Commercial Deployment

Long-term

Commence full-scale plant with reinjection well networks. Target 25–50 MW capacity serving 50,000+ households with reliable, clean baseload electricity.
